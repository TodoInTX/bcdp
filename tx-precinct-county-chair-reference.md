## 1. Introduction

The authority and obligations of precinct chairs and county chairs in Texas derive from three overlapping sources of law:

1. **Texas Election Code** — the primary statutory framework, principally Chapters 171 (county executive committee organization), 172 (primary elections), and 174 (party conventions). These statutes apply to all political parties that hold primary elections in Texas.

2. **Texas Democratic Party (TDP) Rules** — internal party rules that supplement and in some cases expand upon statutory requirements. These rules govern the Democratic Party specifically and are binding on all party officers.

3. **Case Law** — judicial decisions interpreting both the statutes and the interaction between state law and parties' First Amendment rights of association.

Where TDP Rules and the Texas Election Code address the same subject, the Election Code controls on procedural matters unless the Code expressly delegates authority to party rules.

## 2. Texas Election Code — Chapter 171: Organization

> **Official source:** https://statutes.capitol.texas.gov/Docs/EL/htm/EL.171.htm

---

### § 171.021 — County Executive Committee Established

A county executive committee is established for each county in which a primary election is held by a political party.

*Added by Acts 1985, 69th Leg., ch. 211, § 1, eff. Jan. 1, 1986.*

---

### § 171.022 — Committee Composition

**(a)** A county executive committee consists of:

1. a **county chair**; and
2. a **precinct chair** for each county election precinct in the county.

**(b)** The county chair is elected at the general primary election by a majority vote of the qualified voters of the county who vote in the party's primary, or is appointed by the county executive committee in the event of a vacancy.

**(c)** Each precinct chair is elected at the general primary election by a majority vote of the qualified voters of the precinct who vote in the party's primary (subject to § 171.0221), or is appointed by the county executive committee in the event of a vacancy.

**(d)** If no candidate for county chair or precinct chair receives a majority of votes, a runoff is conducted in the same manner as a runoff primary election.

**(e)** Committee members serve for a **term of two years** beginning on the 20th day after runoff primary election day.

**(f)** The state executive committee may adopt rules permitting county chairs or precinct chairs in certain counties to be elected by **plurality vote** rather than majority vote.

*Sources: Texas Election Code § 171.022; texas.public.law/statutes/tex._election_code_section_171.022*

---

### § 171.023 — Residence of Precinct Chair

**(a)** A precinct chair must reside in the county election precinct the chair represents.

**(b)** A change in a county election precinct boundary creates a **vacancy** in the office of precinct chair if:

1. more than one precinct chair resides in the changed precinct after the boundary change; or
2. no precinct chair resides in the changed precinct after the boundary change.

*Source: texas.public.law/statutes/tex._election_code_section_171.023*

---

### § 171.024 — Filling Vacancy

**(a)** A vacancy in the membership of the county executive committee is filled by appointment of the committee, subject to any applicable rules adopted by the state executive committee.

**(b)** To fill a **county chair vacancy**, a majority of the committee's total membership must participate, and the person appointed must receive a favorable vote of a majority of the members voting.

**(c)** To fill a **precinct chair vacancy**, the state executive committee establishes rules specifying the quorum percentage required. The person appointed must receive a favorable vote of a majority of the members voting.

**(d)** A vacancy may not be filled before the term during which the vacancy occurs begins.

**(e)** After filling a vacancy, the county chair must submit the replacement member's name to the **Secretary of State** for posting on the SOS website.

**(f)** The Secretary of State shall establish a system for county chairs to submit this information electronically.

**(g)** The state executive committee may set a deadline for filling vacancies before nomination vacancies arise, but the deadline may not be later than the date the nomination vacancy occurred. This subsection does not apply if the committee has no members.

*Source: texas.public.law/statutes/tex._election_code_section_171.024; codes.findlaw.com/tx/election-code/elec-sect-171-024.html*

---

### § 171.025 — Procedure for Filling Vacancy in Office of County Chair

**(a)** When a vacancy occurs in the office of county chair, the **secretary of the county executive committee** shall call a meeting to fill the vacancy. If a committee member requests the meeting in writing, the secretary must call the meeting within **20 days** of receiving the written request.

**(b)** If the county executive committee has no secretary, or if the secretary fails to call the meeting after a written request from a committee member, the **state chair** shall call the meeting within 20 days of receiving a written request from a committee member.

**(c)** The person calling the meeting must notify all committee members of its **time, place, and purpose** in advance.

**(d)** The authority calling the meeting designates a **temporary chair** from among the committee members to call the meeting to order and preside until the vacancy is filled.

**(e)** In a county with a population of fewer than **5,000**, the state chair may appoint a non-resident as county chair if:
  1. the person resides in an adjacent Texas county with a similar population; and
  2. the Secretary of State approves the appointment.

*Source: texas.public.law/statutes/tex._election_code_section_171.025*

---

### § 171.026 — Proxy Not Allowed

A person may not participate in a county executive committee meeting as a **proxy**.

*Source: texas.public.law/statutes/tex._election_code_section_171.026*

---

### § 171.027 — Temporary Committee

**(a)** If a county lacks an executive committee for a political party that holds a primary election, the party must establish a **temporary county executive committee** under this section.

**(b)** The **state executive committee** or, in certain circumstances, the **state chair** appoints a temporary county chair. If appointed by the state chair, the state executive committee must ratify the appointment or appoint another person at its next regular meeting, by majority vote.

**(c)** The temporary county chair convenes a meeting of county voters affiliated with the party to elect the other temporary committee members. The voters present at the meeting elect the other members.

**(d)** Eligibility requirements for temporary committee members are the same as for regular committee members, except affiliation with the party is not required.

**(e)** A temporary county executive committee has **all authority and performs all duties** of a regularly constituted county executive committee.

**(f)** A permanent county executive committee is elected at the general primary election. Temporary members serve until the elected members assume office.

*Source: law.justia.com/codes/texas/election-code/title-10/subtitle-b/chapter-171/subchapter-b/section-171-027/*

---

### § 171.029 — Removal of Precinct Chair or County Chair for Abandonment of Office

**(a)** A precinct chair or county chair may be removed for **abandonment of office** if the chair:

1. has failed to perform the statutory duties of the office; or
2. has failed to attend **four or more consecutive meetings** of the county executive committee.

**(b) Notice to Precinct Chair:** If authorized by a resolution of the county executive committee, the **county chair** may send written notice to a precinct chair stating that the chair has abandoned the office. The notice must:
  - state the reasons the committee believes the chair has abandoned the office; and
  - request a response not later than the **seventh day** after the precinct chair receives the notice.

**(c) Notice to County Chair:** If authorized by a resolution of the state executive committee, the **state chair** may send written notice to a county chair stating that the chair has abandoned the office. The notice must:
  - state the reasons believed for abandonment; and
  - request a response not later than the seventh day after the county chair receives the notice.

**(d)** A precinct or county chair who receives such a notice must respond within seven days and **affirmatively state** whether they wish to continue in office. **Failure to respond** and affirmatively state the wish to remain in office constitutes a **vacancy** in the office, filled pursuant to § 171.024 (precinct chair) or § 171.025 (county chair).

*Effective Date: September 1, 2015 (Senate Bill 1072, 84th Legislature)*

*Sources: texas.public.law/statutes/tex._election_code_section_171.029; codes.findlaw.com/tx/election-code/elec-sect-171-029.html*

## 3. Texas Election Code — Chapter 172: Primary Elections

> **Official source:** https://statutes.capitol.texas.gov/Docs/EL/htm/EL.172.htm

---

### § 172.023 — Regular Filing Period (Precinct Chair Candidates)

**(a)** A candidate for any office elected at a primary election, including precinct chair, must file an application with the appropriate authority by **6 p.m. on the second Monday in December of an odd-numbered year** (the regular filing deadline).

**(b)** Filing windows by office type:
  - **Standard offices:** Applications may be filed beginning 30 days before the regular filing deadline.
  - **Precinct chair:** Applications may be filed beginning **90 days** before the regular filing deadline (i.e., an earlier opening window).

**(c)** An application for precinct chair **may not be filed earlier** than the 90th day before the regular filing deadline.

**(d)** The application **shall be filed with the county chair** or, if one exists, the secretary of the county executive committee.

*Example (2024 cycle):* For the March 2024 primary, precinct chair applications had to be filed between September 12, 2023 and 6:00 p.m. on December 11, 2023.

*Source: texas.public.law/statutes/tex._election_code_section_172.023; codes.findlaw.com/tx/election-code/elec-sect-172-023.html*

---

### § 172.090 — Separate Ballot for Office of Precinct Chair

In a primary election, the county executive committee may provide by **resolution, order, or other official action** for voting by **separate paper ballot** for the office of precinct chair.

The separate paper ballot for precinct chair must conform to applicable standards governing regular paper ballots, except that the ballot is limited to the office of precinct chair.

The **Secretary of State** prescribes the form of the ballot for precinct chair consistent with this section.

*Source: texas.public.law/statutes/tex._election_code_section_172.090; codes.findlaw.com/tx/election-code/elec-sect-172-090.html*

---

### § 172.116 — Local Canvass

The **county chair** (or a designee) and, if available, at least one member of the county executive committee selected by the committee, shall **canvass the precinct election returns** for the county.

If a designee conducts the canvass on the county chair's behalf, the designee must be:

1. a member of the county executive committee; **or**
2. if no committee member is available, a **resident of the county** served by the committee who is affiliated with the same political party.

*Source: law.justia.com/codes/texas/2019/election-code/title-10/subtitle-b/chapter-172/subchapter-e/section-172-116/*

---

### § 172.118 — Notice of Persons Elected as Party Officers

Not later than the **20th day** after the date the local canvass is completed, the **county chair** shall post on the Secretary of State's website the names of persons elected as:

- county chair; and
- precinct chairs for the county.

Any appointment to fill a vacancy in the office of precinct or county chair shall also be posted on the SOS website.

The notice must include:
1. each party officer's **address**;
2. each precinct chair's **precinct number**; and
3. each precinct officer's **phone number and email address**, if supplied by the officer.

*Sources: law.justia.com/codes/texas/2023/election-code/title-10/subtitle-b/chapter-172/subchapter-e/section-172-118/; codes.findlaw.com/tx/election-code/elec-sect-172-118.html*

## 4. Texas Election Code — Chapter 174: Conventions

> **Official source:** https://statutes.capitol.texas.gov/Docs/EL/htm/EL.174.htm

---

### § 174.025 — Organizing the Precinct Convention

**(a)** The **state executive committee** may adopt a rule requiring the precinct chair to be the **permanent chair** of the precinct convention unless the precinct chair is absent or declines the position. If no such rule is adopted, the precinct chair serves as **temporary chair**.

**(b)** Specific duties of the precinct chair at a precinct convention:

1. **Calling to order:** The precinct chair shall call the convention to order.
2. **Preparing the participant list:** Before conducting any convention business, the precinct chair shall prepare a list containing the **name and residence address** of each person admitted to participate.
3. **Handling absence:** If the precinct chair is absent, any person eligible to participate in the convention may act as temporary chair.
4. **Selection of other officers:** The convention shall select a convention chair (if the precinct chair is not the permanent chair) and a convention secretary. The convention may select any other officers it considers necessary.

*Sources: law.justia.com/codes/texas/2019/election-code/title-10/subtitle-b/chapter-174/subchapter-b/section-174-025/; statutes.capitol.texas.gov/Docs/EL/htm/EL.174.HTM*

## 5. Texas Democratic Party Rules

> **Official source (2024 TDP Rules):** https://docs.google.com/document/d/1vBuxQPp5MEDzl6ofmlKDxv5eeqdPJVY6bQ5w-K9V_xc/

*Note: The TDP Rules supplement the Texas Election Code for Democratic Party officers. Where the Rules and the Code conflict, the Code controls on procedural matters unless the Code expressly delegates authority to the party.*

---

### Article IV — Party Officers, Public Officers, & Candidates

#### Section B — Residency Requirement

> "All Party Officers and candidates for party office shall be residents of the precinct, district or other political subdivision which they represent, and **moving residence outside the precinct, district or other political subdivision shall constitute an automatic vacancy**."

#### Section D — Support of Nominees Pledge

> "Acceptance by any person of a Party Office and participation in that capacity constitutes an agreement to the provision in the Statement of Principles that 'every person who accepts a Party Office at any level (including the positions of Convention Delegate and member of a temporary convention committee) must agree to **support all of the Party's nominees** or shall be subject to removal.'"

---

### Article V — Meetings

#### Section A — General Meeting Requirement

> "Committee meetings shall be held as required by law or by these Rules and called by the Committee Chair or by a petition in writing signed by at least a majority of the Committee membership."

#### Section B — Quorum for Precinct Chair Vacancy Meeting

> "If the meeting is to fill a vacancy in the office of precinct chair, the quorum shall be **25% of the committee membership**."

*Note: For a county chair vacancy, a majority of committee membership must participate (per Texas Election Code § 171.024(b)).*

#### Section F — No Proxy Voting in Local Committees

> "No proxy voting shall be permitted at meetings of any County, District or Precinct Executive Committee."

*This mirrors Texas Election Code § 171.026.*

---

### Article VI — Executive Committees

#### Section A.3.1 — Removal for Endorsing Opposing Party

A party officer may be removed if the officer:

> "publicly supports or endorses an opposing party or nominee of an opposing party, a person seeking the nomination of an opposing party, or a non-Democratic candidate."

#### Section A.3.3.b — Who Receives Complaints

> "Complaints concerning a **County Chair** shall be filed with the **State Chair**. Complaints concerning other Party Officers (except convention delegates) shall be filed with the **County Chair**."

This means complaints about a precinct chair are filed with the county chair.

#### Section A.3.3.c — Notice and Response Procedure

Upon receiving a complaint, the appropriate Chair must:

> "notify the subject Party Officer by **certified mail, return receipt requested, deliver to addressee only**, stating the nature of the complaint."

The subject party officer has a **10-day written response period**. Failure to respond within 10 days results in an **automatic vacancy declaration**.

Either party (the complainant or the subject officer) may request **State Chair review** within 10 days of the county chair's determination.

#### Section B.2 — Majority Vote Requirement for Election to Party Office

> "A **majority vote** of the applicable county, district, or precinct executive committee is required for election to the Party office or to receive the Party's nomination."

> "If no candidate achieves a majority on the first vote, **a runoff vote shall be conducted immediately**."

#### Section C — No Meetings During Conventions

> "No meeting of any executive committee at any level (county, district, or precinct) shall be conducted on a date when any Democratic Party Convention (including the Democratic National Convention) is being held."

*The document also references a prohibition on meetings the day before or after conventions being held in adjacent counties.*

---

### Article VIII — County Executive Committee

#### Section A — Membership

> The County Executive Committee consists of "**all precinct chairs within the county**, the county chair, and the county officers."

#### Section B — Duty to Comply

> County Executive Committees must fulfill their obligations "**in compliance with the law and with the Party Rules**."

#### Section C — Acting Precinct Chair

> "If an elected precinct chair fails or refuses to perform the duties of the office, the **county chair may appoint an acting precinct chair**."

#### Section D — Voting Eligibility on Replacements

> Only "**precinct chairs whose precincts are wholly or partially within**" the relevant geographic boundaries may vote on county chair or candidate vacancy replacements.

#### Section E — Precinct Boundary Changes

> "When precinct boundaries change, the **county chair shall notify all affected precinct chairs**."

#### Section F — Removal for Abandonment (Party Rule)

> "A precinct chair or county chair who **abandons the office by failing to perform the duties of the office for 90 consecutive days** may be removed."

*Compare with Texas Election Code § 171.029, which allows removal for 4 or more consecutive missed meetings.*

#### Section G — Officers

> The County Executive Committee "shall elect a **county chair** and other officers as provided by law."

#### Section H — Meetings

> "Meetings shall be held as required by law or as called by the **county chair** or by **petition signed by a majority of the precinct chairs**."

#### Section I — Compensation

> County chairs and precinct chairs "may receive **reasonable compensation from the primary fund**."

Specific compensation amounts are determined locally by each county executive committee.

#### Section J — Fund Expenditure

> The County Executive Committee "shall expend funds **only for purposes authorized by law and Party Rules**."

#### Section K — Local Rulemaking Authority

> "County Executive Committees may adopt **rules consistent with State Party Rules**."

## 6. Case Law

---

### Rector v. Tarrant County Republican Party (2024)

**Court:** Tarrant County District Court → Second Court of Appeals (Ft. Worth) → Seventh Court of Appeals (Amarillo); Texas Supreme Court (stay order)

**Facts:**
Chris Rector won a general primary election for precinct chair of Tarrant County Republican Precinct 4230, receiving approximately 75% of the vote. Approximately one week after his election victory, party Chairman Bo French declared Rector ineligible for the office and refused to issue a certificate of election. Rector alleged the party chair "concocted a bogus, fraudulent claim that Contestant was ineligible for the position to which he had been elected."

**Legal Issues:**
1. Whether a political party's **First Amendment right of association** permits the party to override a primary election result and declare an elected precinct chair ineligible.
2. The scope of judicial review of internal party decisions about officer eligibility.

**Proceedings:**
- **Trial Court (Tarrant County):** Rector filed suit; initial proceedings favored some level of judicial oversight.
- **Second Court of Appeals:** Seven Republican justices recused themselves due to party affiliation conflicts. The appeal was transferred to the **Seventh Court of Appeals in Amarillo**.
- **Texas Supreme Court (Sept. 6, 2024):** Issued a **stay order** allowing the Tarrant County Republican Party to fill the contested precinct chair position while litigation continues. The Court did not resolve the merits.
- **Status (as of 2024):** Two active proceedings remain — the underlying merits dispute in the trial court and a motion-to-dismiss appeal in the Seventh Court of Appeals. The Tarrant County Republican Party has petitioned the Texas Supreme Court to overturn lower court decisions, arguing that judicial interference violates the party's First Amendment right to freedom of association.

**Party's Position:**
Party general counsel Tony McDonald stated the organization will "fight at every level necessary to defend the First Amendment rights of Tarrant County Republicans."

**Significance:**
This case has the potential to establish significant precedent on whether Texas courts may review a political party's determination of precinct chair eligibility when the party's decision overrides a completed primary election result. The outcome will affect the balance between:
- **Individual candidates'** rights following an election victory;
- **Political parties'** constitutional rights to internal self-governance and freedom of association.

*Sources: KERA News (Sept. 9, 2024); Texas Scorecard (Sept. 2024)*

## 7. Quick Reference Summary

### Precinct Chair — Key Facts

| Topic | Rule |
|---|---|
| **Election** | Elected at general primary by majority vote of precinct voters; runoff if no majority |
| **Term** | 2 years, beginning 20th day after runoff primary election day |
| **Filing deadline** | By 6 p.m. on 2nd Monday in December of odd-numbered year; application window opens 90 days before deadline |
| **Filed with** | County chair or committee secretary |
| **Separate ballot** | County executive committee may authorize separate paper ballot for precinct chair |
| **Residency** | Must reside in precinct represented; change of residence outside precinct = automatic vacancy |
| **Role at precinct convention** | Temporary chair (or permanent chair if state exec committee rule so provides); must call convention to order and prepare participant list |
| **Role on county exec committee** | Voting member |
| **Proxy** | May not participate by proxy |
| **Compensation** | May receive reasonable compensation from primary fund (amount set locally) |

### County Chair — Key Facts

| Topic | Rule |
|---|---|
| **Election** | Elected at general primary by majority vote of county voters; runoff if no majority |
| **Vacancy election** | County executive committee fills vacancy; majority of total membership must participate; majority of voting members must approve |
| **Procedure for vacancy meeting** | Secretary calls meeting within 20 days of written request; if no secretary or failure to call, state chair calls it |
| **Temporary chair at vacancy meeting** | Designated from among committee members by the authority calling the meeting |
| **Small-county exception** | In counties under 5,000 population, state chair may appoint non-resident chair from adjacent county (with SOS approval) |
| **Canvass duty** | Conducts or designates a member to conduct the local canvass of primary returns |
| **Notice duty** | Posts elected officers to SOS website within 20 days after local canvass |
| **Removal (statutory)** | By state executive committee for abandonment (failure to perform duties or 4+ consecutive missed meetings); 7-day notice/response |
| **Removal (TDP rules)** | For endorsing opposing party/non-Democratic candidate; complaint to State Chair; certified mail notice; 10-day response |
| **Acting precinct chair** | May appoint acting precinct chair if elected chair fails/refuses to perform duties |

### County Executive Committee — Key Facts

| Topic | Rule |
|---|---|
| **Composition** | All precinct chairs in the county + county chair (+ county officers under TDP Rules) |
| **Quorum (general)** | Set by state executive committee rules (TDP: 40% for general meetings) |
| **Quorum (precinct chair vacancy)** | TDP Rules: 25% of membership |
| **Quorum (county chair vacancy)** | Majority of total membership must participate (Tex. Elec. Code § 171.024(b)) |
| **Proxy** | Prohibited (both by statute and TDP rules) |
| **Removal authority** | May pass resolution authorizing county chair to send abandonment notice to precinct chairs |
| **Local rules** | May adopt rules consistent with TDP State Party Rules |
| **Convention restriction** | May not meet on date any Democratic Party Convention is being held |
| **Fund authority** | May compensate chairs from primary fund; may only expend funds for authorized purposes |
| **Election of officers** | Elects county chair and other officers as provided by law |
| **Acting appointments** | County chair may appoint acting precinct chair when elected chair fails/refuses to serve |

### Vacancy Triggers

| Event | Effect |
|---|---|
| Precinct chair moves outside precinct | Automatic vacancy |
| Precinct boundary change → no chair resides in precinct | Vacancy |
| Precinct boundary change → more than one chair resides in precinct | Vacancy |
| Failure to respond to abandonment notice within 7 days (statute) | Vacancy |
| Failure to respond to complaint notice within 10 days (TDP rules) | Automatic vacancy |
| Endorsing opposing party/non-Democratic candidate | Grounds for removal → vacancy |
| Failure to perform duties for 90 consecutive days (TDP rules) | Grounds for removal → vacancy |

### Removal — Statutory vs. Party Rule Comparison

| Mechanism | Statute (§ 171.029) | TDP Rules (Art. VI, VIII) |
|---|---|---|
| **Grounds** | Failure to perform duties; 4+ consecutive missed meetings | Endorsing opposing party; failure to perform duties 90+ days |
| **For precinct chair** | County exec committee resolution → county chair sends notice | Complaint to county chair |
| **For county chair** | State exec committee resolution → state chair sends notice | Complaint to state chair |
| **Notice method** | Written notice with stated reasons | Certified mail, return receipt, addressee only |
| **Response deadline** | 7 days | 10 days |
| **Effect of no response** | Vacancy | Automatic vacancy |
| **Appeal** | Not specified in § 171.029 | Either party may request state chair review within 10 days |

## Appendix A: Framework for County Local Rules

### Authority

Under **TDP Rules Article VIII, Section K**:

> "County Executive Committees may adopt rules consistent with State Party Rules."

And under **Texas Election Code § 171.022(f)**:

> "The state executive committee may establish rules permitting county chairs or precinct chairs in certain counties to be elected by plurality vote rather than majority vote."

### What County Parties Are Authorized to Regulate Locally

The following topics are within the scope of county executive committee rulemaking authority, provided no local rule conflicts with TDP State Party Rules or Texas Election Code:

| Subject Area | Scope of Local Authority |
|---|---|
| **Quorum** | May set quorum percentages for general meetings (TDP sets floor: 25% for precinct chair vacancy meetings; majority for county chair vacancy per § 171.024(b)) |
| **Meeting frequency** | May require meetings more frequently than state law minimum; may specify notice periods |
| **Meeting format** | May authorize electronic/videoconference meetings |
| **Standing committees** | May establish standing committees (e.g., Rules, Finance, Grievance, Campaign, Communications); may require committee chairs to be precinct chairs |
| **Vacancy committees** | May create sub-committee structures (e.g., by Congressional District) to vet and nominate candidates for precinct chair vacancies |
| **Endorsements** | May adopt a non-partisan endorsement policy for races where two Democrats are not competing; may prohibit endorsements in primaries |
| **Conflict of interest** | May require disclosure and recusal for precinct chairs receiving compensation from parties interested in CEC votes |
| **Bylaw amendments** | May set supermajority requirements and advance notice periods for amending local bylaws |
| **Emergency resolutions** | May set procedures (e.g., supermajority vote) for considering resolutions without prior notice |
| **Officer conduct / discipline** | May establish a Rules or Grievance Committee to investigate complaints and recommend discipline to the CEC |
| **Acting precinct chair** | May define county chair's authority to appoint acting chairs when elected chairs fail to perform duties (permitted by TDP Art. VIII §C) |
| **Compensation** | May authorize and set amounts of compensation for chairs from the primary fund (TDP Art. VIII §I) |
| **Fund expenditures** | May set approval processes for budget and expenditures (subject to law and TDP Rules) |
| **Super precincts** | Some counties (e.g., Travis) authorize county chairs to form "super precincts" aggregating small precincts and appoint super precinct chairs |
| **Plurality voting** | In authorized counties, may adopt plurality rather than majority for chair elections (subject to state exec committee rule) |

### Limits on Local Rulemaking

Local rules are **void** if they:
- Conflict with any provision of the Texas Election Code;
- Conflict with TDP State Party Rules;
- Conflict with federal or state law; or
- Were not adopted by vote of the county executive committee.

> "Any provision in county bylaws determined to be in conflict with federal, state, or local law or the Rules of the Texas Democratic Party shall be null and void."
> *(Standard conflict-of-law clause adopted in multiple county bylaws)*

## Appendix E: Harris County Democratic Party

**Document:** Harris County Democratic Party Rules and Bylaws
**Last Amended:** March 23, 2025
**Official URL:** https://www.harrisdemocrats.org/rules-bylaws

### Precinct Chairs

**Election & Term:**
- Elected every two years in Democratic Primary elections
- Each precinct chair represents their voting precinct and serves on the CEC
- Each precinct chair is the chief organizer for Democrats in their precinct

**Abandonment / Removal:**
Per Texas Election Code § 171.029(a), moving outside the election precinct or failing to attend four consecutive CEC meetings constitutes abandonment of office. Upon a determination of abandonment:
- County chair may delete the precinct chair's name from the official Texas Secretary of State website
- County chair shall notify the former precinct chair that they are no longer eligible to serve on the CEC

**Vacancy Filling:**
- Each Congressional District has a **Vacancy Committee** of at least 6 members
- The Congressional District Chair names the Vacancy Committee Chair
- The Vacancy Committee nominates qualified persons to fill CEC vacancies
- All nominations are subject to **majority approval** of the full County Executive Committee

### County Chair

**Duties:**
- Presides over all CEC meetings
- Coordinates with precinct chairs across Harris County
- Plans and hosts the County Convention
- Recruits, trains, and supports candidates and precinct chairs
- Mobilizes volunteers for voter registration, outreach, and GOTV efforts
- Raises money to fund the organization
- Serves as party spokesperson

**Vacancy on Standing Committees:**
Vacancies caused by death, resignation, or legal disqualification are filled in the same manner used to select the person who vacated the position.

### County Executive Committee

**Quorum:**
> "Twenty-five percent (25%) of the members (exclusive of any vacancies) of the CEC shall constitute a quorum."

**Meeting Procedures:**
> "Conducted in accordance with **Robert's Rules of Order Newly Revised** except as otherwise provided herein or in the Rules of the Texas Democratic Party (TDP Rules), and applicable laws of the State of Texas."

**Closed Meetings:**
Portions of CEC meetings may be closed to the public when discussing:
- Financial, legal, or strategic matters not previously publicly revealed
- Proprietary information
- Personnel documents
- Disciplinary matters

Closed sessions are limited to committee members, HCDP staff, and individuals invited to present.

**Standing Committees:**
1. **Rules Committee** — Prepares rules for meetings, reviews and proposes changes to Rules and Bylaws; also receives, investigates, and adjudicates complaints against precinct chairs
2. **Finance / Fundraising Committee** — Reviews budget and recommends changes
3. **Primary Committee** — Administers the party's Primary
4. **Steering Committee** — Serves as Advisory Committee to County Chair; acts on behalf of CEC between regular meetings

### Endorsements

> "Harris County Democratic Party does NOT endorse candidates in primary elections."

- Any candidate who seeks or receives a primary endorsement knowing it will create a false impression of HCDP endorsement:
  - Will not be allowed to address any HCDP meeting until the primary (including runoffs) concludes
  - Cannot use the HCDP website to promote their candidacy
- Democratic clubs may endorse in primaries but must ensure the public is not misled into thinking the endorsement is from HCDP itself
- Clubs must refuse to endorse (or withdraw endorsement) if a candidate is endorsed by any entity using a name deceptively similar to "Harris County Democratic Party" or "HCDP," unless the candidate promptly disavows it

### Officer Conduct & Discipline

The **Rules Committee**:
- Receives, investigates, and considers complaints against precinct chairs
- Conducts hearings affording accused precinct chairs a fair opportunity to be heard
- After hearing, makes recommendations to the CEC concerning appropriate discipline for violations of HCDP Rules, TDP Rules, or State Law

*Note: The full bylaws are hosted as a restricted Google Doc. The provisions below reflect publicly available information from the TCDP website.*

### Precinct Chairs

**Qualifications:**
- Must be at least 18 years old
- Must be a registered voter in the precinct
- Must reside in the precinct they represent

**Election & Appointment:**

- Assist in recruiting election judges

**CEC Membership:** "Precinct Chairs are the Travis County Democratic Party leaders in your neighborhood" and are voting members of the CEC.

### County Executive Committee

**Composition:** Every Travis County precinct chair + the County Chair

**Meeting Schedule:** Monthly (except in March and June in election years when County and State Conventions are held, and in December)

**Authority:** Operates under the Rules of the Democratic Party of Texas; may adopt additional local rules consistent with TDP Rules.

**Super Precincts:** The County Chair has authority to approve the formation of Super Precincts (aggregating very small precincts) and appoint Super Precinct Chairs.

### Non-Partisan Endorsement Policy

> *Source: https://www.traviscountydemocrats.org/nonpartisan-endorsement-policy*

- **Scope:** TCDP may endorse only in **non-partisan races** where the race is between one Democrat and a non-Democrat. Endorsements are explicitly **prohibited** in elections with multiple Democratic candidates.
- **Democratic status:** Qualifies as a Democrat by voting in the most recent Democratic Primary or signing an official Oath of Affiliation under the Texas Election Code.
- **Two-step endorsement process:**
  - *Step One:* Requires a 2/3 vote to suspend rules AND an 85% approval vote to endorse at the initial meeting.
  - *Step Two:* Consideration vote at the subsequent CEC meeting.
- **Eligibility to vote on endorsements:** Only precinct chairs elected in the previous primary or appointed **at least 45 days** before the vote may participate.
- **Conflict of interest:** Precinct chairs and TCDP employees must disclose any compensation received from interested parties and **recuse themselves** from relevant votes.
- **Ballot measures:** The CEC may also take positions on propositions and local jurisdiction agenda items upon written request from jurisdictional residents.

- The party is led by the County Chair, elected in even-year primaries
- Chair is supported by professional staff: Executive Director, office assistant, communications manager, coordinated campaign director
